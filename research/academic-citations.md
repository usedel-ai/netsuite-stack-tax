## AI Productivity & The J-Curve

**Brynjolfsson, E., Rock, D., & Syverson, C. (2021)**
"The Productivity J-Curve: How Intangibles Complement General Purpose Technologies"
*American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics*, Vol. 13, No. 1, pp. 333–372

Key finding: Official total factor productivity (TFP) measures understated AI-driven productivity gains by **15.9%** during 2005–2016 because GAAP accounting cannot capture investment in intangible complements (data infrastructure, process redesign, organizational capital).

Implication for ERP migration: When a CFO says "we see no ROI from AI," the accounting system is the problem — not the AI investment.

## GAAP and the Dead Capital Problem

**Lev, B., & Radhakrishnan, S. (2003)**
"The Valuation of Organization Capital"
*NBER Working Paper No. 9581*

Key finding: GAAP ASC 350 requires that internally generated intangible assets — including software developed for internal use, process automations, and AI workflows — be **expensed immediately**, not capitalized. They appear as costs on the income statement, never as assets on the balance sheet.

Implication: Every AI workflow your team built this year is legally invisible on your balance sheet. Your accounting system prohibits recognizing it as the asset it is.

## Digital Capital Concentration

**Tambe, P., Hitt, L., Rock, D., & Brynjolfsson, E. (2021)**
"Digital Capital and Superstar Firms"
*NBER Working Paper No. 28285*

Key findings:
- Digital capital Gini coefficient: **~0.90** (extreme concentration)
- Physical capital Gini: **~0.60** (significantly lower)
- Top 1% of firms ("superstar firms") hold **~40%** of all digital capital
- Top 10% hold **~90%**
- Bottom 90% (mid-market) hold **~10%**

Implication: Mid-market companies not actively building digital capital are falling behind in a compounding way. The gap between superstar firms and mid-market is not narrowing — it's widening.

## ERP Project Economics

**Panorama Consulting (2024)**
"2024 ERP Report"

Key findings relevant to mid-market ERP evaluation:
- Most common cause of ERP project overruns: unexpected technology needs
- Average ERP implementation runs over budget and over schedule
- Total cost of ownership significantly underestimated at contract signing

Note: Panorama surveys ERP implementations across company sizes. Mid-market data ($30M–$300M revenue) directionally consistent with del.ai's research but not isolated in the public report.

## Platform Economics

**Rochet, J.C., & Tirole, J. (2006)**
"Two-Sided Markets: A Progress Report"
*RAND Journal of Economics*, Vol. 37, No. 3, pp. 645–667

Key finding: Two-sided platforms rationally charge one side below cost (sometimes zero) to attract the other side, which they charge at a premium. The value created by network effects justifies asymmetric pricing.

Implication for del.ai marketplace model: The economic logic behind paying engineers royalties on deployed agents while charging companies for migration is validated by Nobel Prize-winning platform theory.
