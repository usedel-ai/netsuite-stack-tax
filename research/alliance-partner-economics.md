# Alliance Partner Economics — Why Your Consultant Won't Show You This Math

*By del.ai — June 2026*

---

## How Alliance Partners Get Paid

NetSuite sells through a partner channel. Alliance Partners — consulting firms certified by Oracle to implement and support NetSuite — earn revenue two ways:

1. **Implementation fees** — time-and-materials or fixed-price project work
2. **ACV commission** — a percentage of your Annual Contract Value at renewal, paid by Oracle

The second one is the problem.

When your Alliance Partner manages your renewal negotiation, they earn a commission on the ACV you agree to. A higher renewal price means a higher commission. They have no financial incentive to push back on Oracle's proposed uplift.

The exact commission rates are not published. Based on partner program documentation and community discussions, estimates range from 10% to 25% of ACV depending on partner tier.

On a $120k ACV renewal, that's $12k–$30k going to your partner. Per year. Every year you stay.

---

## What This Creates

**Structural misalignment.** Your Alliance Partner is retained to advise you on your NetSuite spend. The entity paying them to do that advisory work is Oracle — indirectly, through the renewal commission.

This is not fraud. It is a business model. But it means:

- Your partner will not proactively show you a 5-year cost model
- Your partner will not suggest you evaluate alternatives at renewal time
- Your partner earns more as your stack grows (more SuiteApps = more ACV = higher commission base)
- Your partner has a financial interest in the complexity of your implementation

The Alliance Partner is not your adversary. They are a rational actor responding to their incentive structure.

---

## The Retainer Line Item

Most mid-market NetSuite deployments pay a separate Alliance Partner retainer for ongoing support: $30k–$100k/yr depending on deployment complexity and support level.

This retainer is separate from the ACV commission. It appears on your books. The commission does not — Oracle pays it directly to the partner.

So the true cost of your Alliance Partner relationship is:
- Retainer (visible): $30k–$100k/yr
- ACV commission (invisible, paid by Oracle): estimated $12k–$30k/yr on $120k ACV

The invisible portion never appears in your spend analysis.
